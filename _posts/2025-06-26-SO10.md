## U(n) in SO(2n)

## Clifford Algebra

The [geometric algebra](https://niklasbuschmann.github.io/multiplying-vectors/) in \\( 2n \\) dimensions has \\( 2^{2n}\\) degrees of freedom, so its matrix representation must consist of \\( 2^n \times 2^n \\) matrices. One representation can be found using the [Weyl-Brauer matrices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weyl–Brauer_matrices), here for \\( 2n = 10 \\) dimensions:

$$
  \gamma_1=\bm{\sigma_x} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \qquad \gamma_2=\bm{\sigma_y} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \\
  \gamma_3=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_x} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \qquad \gamma_4=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_y} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \\
  \gamma_5=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_x} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \qquad \gamma_6=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_y} \otimes \bm{1} \otimes \bm{1} \\
  \gamma_7=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes  \bm{\sigma_x} \otimes \bm{1} \qquad \gamma_8=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes  \bm{\sigma_y} \otimes \bm{1} \\
  \gamma_9=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes  \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_x} \qquad \gamma_{10}=\bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes  \bm{\sigma_z} \otimes \bm{\sigma_y}
$$

Where the \\( \bm{\sigma_i} \\) are the [Pauli matrices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pauli_matrices) and \\( \otimes \\) is the [tensor product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kronecker_product):

$$
  \bm{\sigma_x} = \begin{pmatrix}0 & 1\\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix} \quad \bm{\sigma_y} = \begin{pmatrix}0 & -i\\ i & 0\end{pmatrix} \quad \bm{\sigma_z} = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0\\ 0 & -1\end{pmatrix}
$$
